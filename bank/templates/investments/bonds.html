About Bond Investing

Bonds are debt securities where investors loan money to an entity (corporate or governmental) for a defined period at a fixed or variable interest rate. Bonds are generally considered more stable than stocks and provide regular income through interest payments. However, they typically offer lower returns than stocks over the long term. Bonds can be a valuable part of a diversified portfolio, particularly for investors seeking income or looking to reduce overall portfolio risk.

Treasury Bonds

Issued by the federal government with maturities of 10+ years. They're considered among the safest investments available.

Corporate Bonds

Issued by companies to fund operations. They typically offer higher yields than government bonds but carry more risk.

Municipal Bonds

Issued by state and local governments. Interest is often exempt from federal and sometimes state taxes.
